Learning path

Training modules (3 levels)

Progressive skills uplift from fundamentals to expertise with certification at each step.

Level 1: Fundamentals (5 days)

Audience: New entrants, career switchers, project managers

  • Telecom networking concepts (CS/PS, RRC/NAS protocols, mobility)
  • 2G->5G RAN architecture (NodeB, eNodeB, gNodeB, fronthaul/backhaul)
  • Mobile core (MSC, SGSN, MME, AMF, standard interfaces)
  • Essential KPIs (CSSR, CDR, throughput, latency, availability)

Certification: 50-question MCQ, 80% pass mark

Level 2: Operational (10 days)

Audience: Junior engineers, senior technicians, technical project managers

  • RF planning (link budget, Okumura-Hata/COST231 propagation models)
  • Optimisation (drive-tests, parameter tuning, degraded KPI analysis)
  • Troubleshooting (OMC log analysis, Wireshark traces, root cause analysis)
  • Real-case projects (LTE cluster dimensioning, 5G SA rollout)

Certification: Individual practical project + presentation to a jury

Level 3: Expert (15 days)

Audience: Senior engineers, architects, technical leads, directors

  • Multi-vendor E2E architecture (Ericsson/Huawei/Nokia, interoperability)
  • Advanced SON (MLB/MRO/CCO, proprietary algorithms, machine learning)
  • 5G standalone: slicing, MEC, URLLC, private networks
  • CAPEX/OPEX business case, 3-5 year strategic roadmaps

Certification: 30-page technical paper + defence before an expert panel

Transformation tracking

Adoption indicators (5 phases)

Progressive measurement of skills uplift with business KPIs for training ROI.

D+0: Baseline & D+30: Training

  • D+0: Initial skills audit (diagnostic test, gap mapping vs needs)
  • D+30: Participation rate ≥90%, post-training satisfaction ≥4/5
  • Certifications obtained vs targets (e.g. 80% level 1, 50% level 2)

D+60: Application & D+120: Autonomy

  • D+60: Pilot project tracking with defined KPIs (e.g. cut CDR >2% by 30%)
  • D+120: Teams able to repeat methodologies without external support
  • Indicators: incident resolution time (-40%), deliverable quality (peer review ≥3.5/5)

D+180: Continuous improvement

  • Local innovation: new use cases, optimisations specific to client context
  • Internal knowledge sharing (wiki, REX, junior mentoring by seniors)
  • Maintained certification (annual re-assessment, continuous training)

Structured handover

Skills transfer (4 steps)

Proven method for progressive client team autonomy.

1. Shadowing (Weeks 1-2)

  • Client teams observe GWIT consultants on real field missions
  • Explanation of decisions, tools used, methodologies applied
  • Live documentation: shared templates, checklists, scripts

2. Co-piloting (Weeks 3-6)

  • Mixed pairs: client leads, GWIT consultant supervises and corrects in real time
  • Daily debriefs: strengths, areas to improve, best practices
  • Intermediate validation: mini-projects with formative assessment

3-4. Supervised autonomy → Remote support

  • Weeks 7-10: Client runs solo, weekly reviews with GWIT
  • Months 4-6: Remote support — technical hotline, Q&A, best-practice sharing
  • Deliverables: internal wiki, screencast videos of procedures, Python/MATLAB toolbox
